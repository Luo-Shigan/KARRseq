Plotting Contactmaps¶

Using these functions, contact maps can be constructed from the pairs files to visualize your transcripts/isoforms of interest. First, we visualize the differences among the following methods (KARR-seq, PARIS and RIC-seq) by looking at the Telomerase RNA component, TERC, transcript. By proving a dot-bracket notation file, we can overlap secondary structures onto the contact map. Here, we use the known secondary structure obtained from Rfam to illustrate.

Clustering chimeric reads and classifying them into loops, left stripes and right stripes¶

To cluster the chimeric groups, we first define a similarity function by counting the amount of overlapd between two chimeric pairs. We can then construct the graph by building the similarity matrix, and then cluster the chimeric reads into chimeric groups by using a greedy modularity algorithm. We can then classify the chimeric groups into loops, left stripes and right stripes.

Using a BLAST analysis, even though they have a 91.4% precentage identity, their general topologies are similar, suggesting that structure are conserved and plausibly functional.
